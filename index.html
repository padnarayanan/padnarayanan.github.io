An AI leader who has carried P&L, shipped at scale, and earned the boardroom.

Twenty-plus years moving from architect to senior executive across Japan, India, California, and Georgia, with a track record of translating advanced technologies into measurable business outcomes for global manufacturers, insurers, and hospitality operators.

Today I serve as Global Head of AI at Mohawk Industries, where I partner with the CEO, C-suite, and Board to set AI strategy, governance, and operating cadence for a 43,000-person enterprise. The mandate is broad: predictive analytics, Vision AI in plants, MLOps, custom LLMs and Generative AI, and the platform spine that keeps it all production-grade.

I came up the long way. Mechanical engineering and tool-and-die training in India. CNC and robotics R&D at Makino in Tokyo for nearly a decade. Application architecture and team leadership at Keenan & Associates on the West Coast. Then the climb at Mohawk from Architect to VP to Global Head of AI in five years, with a Kellogg MBA and an Illinois Tech MS along the way.

Outside the day job, I sit on the Board of Hemophilia of Georgia, hold a Fellowship with the British Computer Society, founded a non-profit (Good and Humble), and operate Color Pencil Technology Inc. I write about AI in manufacturing and hospitality for industry forums, and I serve as judge and advisor to founders building in adjacent spaces. Recognition has followed the work: MachineCon Top AI100, Stand Out 50 Leaders 2025, and Top 25 Atlanta Data & AI Leader 2026 from CDO Magazine.

Based in Atlanta. Reachable for executive AI roles, board engagements, advisory mandates, and the occasional thoughtful conversation.
